Free Beginner Level Trainings

Download Flyer

The San Diego County Office of Education's Educational Technology Department is offering a continuation of our free, online, beginner-level courses for county teachers. Our courses are aimed at creating strong online and hybrid environments for students that increase student/family access to information and resources, anticipate student variation from the onse.0t, and foster rigorous academic inquiry, collaboration, and educational synthesis.


Who can sign up? 
These workshops are geared toward classroom teachers and classroom support staff. While they are not geared toward school office staff or other support staff, these staff members have found benefit in attending and are welcome to attend. 

How do I attend?
Please use the registration link found below. There is a single registration form for selecting classes of interest. Registrants will receive a confirmation email with there choices indicated. Reminder emails are sent to registered participants about a week in advance along with the Zoom link for the meeting. We send out additional reminders on the day of the event for your convenience. 

Do I have to attend all the sessions?
For the most powerful experience, it's recommended that attendees plan to participate in all the sessions. We've planned the sessions to compliment each other through a larger overarching goal of creating a richer online/hybrid environment. However, beginning teachers will learn new skills regardless. Each session creates a single component of this "richer environment".

What do I need to participate?
In order to participate in our hands-on sessions, we highly recommend having either a two-monitor set up, or two different devices so you can easily view and work at the same time. However, this and our other suggestions are by no means a requirement. We also recommend using a laptop or desktop device, rather than a tablet. Having a mouse can help with efficiency when you are creating. Finally, we will be using many Google applications which work best in the Chrome Browser, and some Google Extensions which only work on a Chrome Browser. Installing Chrome in advance and making sure you have a Google account (either through your district or a personal account) is necessary.

Humanize Your Online &
Hybrid Environments

Screencastify with a Google Slides Template
Tuesday, August 17
5 to 6 pm

Whether you are fully online, hybrid, or using other blended learning approaches it's important to have a well-organized and personable space for students to access information. We'll kick off this series by helping you craft an engaging & personalized welcome message for your students.

Learn or revisit some basics of Screencastify and Google Docs while being supported step by step through the process. This is a hands-on class, our goal is for you to leave with a product you created. 

Create Clever Classroom
Banners & Headers

Google Drawings & Bitmoji App
Tuesday, September 21
5 to 6 pm

Ever wonder how other teachers create their own banners for their classroom websites, newsletters, or even Google Forms? Add another layer to your virtual space by learning to create headers and banners in Google Drawings. We'll give you a template so you don't have to guess the dimensions and can start now.

If you already have a Bitmoji account we'll show you how to easily pull in images that add a little bit of fun. Don't have a Bitmoji Account? Don't worry, here's a step by step job aid. (Job Aid: Click Here)

Classroom Resources Hub
& Schedules

Google Docs - Formatting, Tables, & Publishing
Tuesday, October 12
5 to 6 pm

Keeping resources organized and accessible for students and families is a must. We will be walking you through how to create a one-stop shop or "Hub" for all the classroom resources that students/families may use frequently. Also create and customize classroom schedules to be visually appealing and organized. 

Our goal is that you leave with a really good start on this product and an easy plan for how to expand on it. If you are already familiar with Google Docs we are sure that you will still learn something new! 

Build Awareness About
Your Students


Google Forms
Tuesday, October 26
5 to 6 pm

If you are unaware of what is happening in your student’s lives, you can’t truly understand their needs. In online/hybrid environments we have to include structures for learning about our students so that we don’t default to stereotypes, bias, and what you “think”.

Know what's going on in your student's lives by building a Check-in Survey. We walk you through how to build it, you have a form you can use immediately, and you learn about Google Forms in the process. It’s a two-for-one!

Putting Your Resources 
All Together


Google Sites and/or Google Slides
Tuesday, November 16
5 to 6 pm

Now that you've built what you need for a great personalized space, we'll show you how to create a webpage to put it in using Google Sites or Google Slides. Whether to teach in a fully online, hybrid, or face to face environment, having a strong web presence is crucial.

If you have been attending our previous sessions you will have some great elements to place in a website, if not, don’t worry because you’ll learn a lot about how these programs are used to create and publish content. 

Build a Strong Parent/Family
Connection


Talkingpoints
Tuesday, Dec 7
5 to 6 pm

Anytime, anywhere access to classroom information is one way to ensure that parents and students see your classroom as supportive and welcoming. So what elements make up a robust online/hybrid webspace and what other ways can you connect directly with families?
